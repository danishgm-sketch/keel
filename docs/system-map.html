The rule that governs everything

Trading systems rarely fail from lack of cleverness — they fail because they fool themselves into betting on noise. So only a strategy proven out-of-sample may risk money. Everything smart can only choose among proven things, or make the system more careful.

1 · The pipeline

Data flows one way. The two dotted feedbacks below are the only influences that touch trading, and both are safety-bounded.

DataAlpaca market data — bars, snapshots, news
ScanScanner ranks the whole market by liquidity → top candidates + recent bars
ChooseMeta-brain picks the best proven strategy per symbol, per moment
Entry signal?If no → back to scan. If yes → continue
↻ no → re-scan next cycle
Qualitative limb — veto?News / earnings / halt check. If vetoed → stand aside
↻ vetoed → drop the name, re-scan
RiskSize 1% of equity from the stop — a constant, nothing may change it
ExecuteBroker places an Alpaca PAPER order — live money disabled in code
RecordJournal — every decision, append-only
JudgeWalk-forward gate → edge ledger: is there edge, out-of-sample?

↺ Edge ledger → meta-brain

Promotes proven only. A strategy reaches live selection solely by surviving the gate.

↺ AI brain → risk

Tightens only. A defensive posture reduces trades and positions; it can never loosen risk.

2 · Two limbs

Trades are driven by numbers and read against the news — but the limbs have very different authority.

Quantitative — only source of entries

A proven strategy fires a signal, then it is sized 1% from the stop. This is the only thing that can open a trade.

Qualitative — parallel, veto only

Reads news / catalysts and flags event risk. It can only remove a name from consideration — never create a buy.

is this name vetoed?
no → take the tradeyes → stand aside

3 · The brain & its training loop

A local, Keel-only LLM reads all state and sets a risk posture, then learns from what actually happened.

system state→ Keel-only LLM→ posture (tighten-only)→ remember→ grade by outcome→ lessons

↺ lessons fold back into the constitution — it learns to be right, not confident.

4 · The honesty gate

What earns the right to risk money. Most ideas die here — that is the machine working.

a strategy / variant→ in-sample bootstrap→ FDR across ALL variants→ out-of-sample confirm→ walk-forward, many folds→ no → stay defensive yes, repeatedly → earns size

5 · The code, module by module

ModuleIts job, in plain terms
data · universeValidated bars; point-in-time membership. Look-ahead and survivorship bias made structurally impossible.
indicatorsCausal EMA / RSI / ATR — every value uses only the past.
strategiesThe playbook: rsi2 (mean reversion), orb (breakout), swing (trend pullback).
metaPicks the best-fitting strategy per symbol, per moment; re-selects as the regime shifts, never mid-trade.
backtest · portfolioNext-open fills, gap-aware stops, costs on every fill; the multi-symbol engine is the live logic in test form.
risk · costsFixed 1%-from-stop sizing (a constant); a realistic cost model that keeps turnover honest.
statsThe judge — block-bootstrap null + Benjamini–Hochberg. The part that can say “no”.
roster · walkforwardEvolution + the out-of-sample gate; writes the champion and the edge ledger.
scanner · catalysts · overlayWhole-market liquidity ranking, the news feed, and the veto-only qualitative limb.
brain · knowledge · trainingThe Keel-only AI, its constitution, and the outcome-graded training loop.
broker · trader · servicePaper client, the live loop body, and the always-on orchestrator.
ui · app · doctorThe watch-only monitor, the desktop launcher, and one-command honest status.

6 · To study, better, or remove

One rule for every change: does it let the machine risk money on something unproven? If yes, it is wrong — however clever. If no, improve away.

Fair game — improve freely

Add a strategy (strategies.py → VARIANTS), change what “best now” means (meta._score), tune the veto (overlay.py), reshape the UI. Re-validate with keel walkforward.

The spine — do not weaken

The 1%-from-stop sizing in risk.py, the paper-only boundary in broker.py, and the veto-only / tighten-only bounds in overlay.py and brain.py.
